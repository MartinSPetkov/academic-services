Service 01

Academic Writing

For when you have done the research but need the manuscript written. This service produces a complete, well-argued paper from your secondary sources, notes, and materials. Primary fields: business, management, marketing, and leadership, and adjacent areas in organizational studies, strategy, and applied social research.

What this service covers

Writing from your material,
not from scratch

The core challenge in social science writing is not collecting information: it is turning a body of research into a coherent argument. You bring the material. This service builds the manuscript around it.

Depending on what you provide, this can mean writing a paper from existing notes and sources, drafting specific sections where you are stuck, or organizing a collection of evidence into a structure that holds together under scrutiny.

01

Scoping the project

Before any writing begins, we establish the research question or argument, the paper type, the target venue if applicable, and the secondary sources you are working from. This determines the structure and sets the scope.

02

Reviewing and organising the literature

I work through the sources you provide, identifying the relevant threads, mapping the existing scholarship, and establishing where your argument sits within it. For papers requiring a literature review section, this becomes its foundation.

03

Structuring the argument

A paper that cannot be outlined clearly cannot be written well. Before drafting begins, we agree on a structure: the sections, the sequence, and what each part needs to establish for the argument to hold together. If you have an existing outline, this is where we refine it.

04

Writing the draft

I write to the agreed structure using your notes and sources. The draft follows the conventions of social science writing: appropriate citation style, measured claims, evidence-backed reasoning. The voice is scholarly, not generic.

05

Revision rounds

You review the draft and provide feedback. Two revision rounds are included as standard: one for structural or content changes, one for final refinements. Additional rounds can be arranged if needed.

Scope

What this includes,
and what it does not

This service works entirely from secondary sources: published research, existing data, your notes, and materials you provide. It does not include primary research of any kind.

Included

  • Literature review and secondary source analysis
  • Argument development and structure
  • Full manuscript drafting from your notes and sources
  • Section-by-section drafting if you only need part of the paper
  • Introduction and conclusion writing
  • Abstract writing
  • Citation formatting in your required style
  • Two revision rounds as standard

Not included

  • Primary research of any kind: data collection, fieldwork, surveys, interviews
  • Statistical analysis or interpretation beyond what you provide
  • Work your institution requires you to produce independently
  • Source procurement: I work from what you supply

Rate: €0.25 per word of finished draft. All projects are quoted individually before work begins. Send a description of the paper, your target venue, the sources you are working from, any notes you have, and your deadline.

Service 02

Developmental Editing

A rigorous structural review of a complete draft. For papers where the research is sound but the argument is not landing: the logic has gaps, the structure makes reviewers work too hard, or the thesis gets lost somewhere in the middle.

Why papers get rejected

Most rejection is a structure problem,
not a science problem

Peer reviewers are reading quickly and under cognitive load. A manuscript that requires effort to follow does not get the benefit of the doubt. Arguments that are buried, transitions that are missing, sections that repeat earlier content, claims without adequate support: these are fixable problems, but only if you can identify them before submission.

Developmental editing examines the draft at the architectural level, before you polish the language. It finds the issues that matter most and tells you precisely what to do about them.

What gets examined

Seven dimensions of academic structure

Every manuscript is assessed against seven structural criteria. Each finding is prioritized so you know where to spend your revision time.

D1

Thesis and central argument

Is the central claim stated clearly and early enough? Is it specific enough to be meaningfully defended? Does the paper deliver on what the thesis promises, or does the argument drift? Does the conclusion return to the thesis in light of the evidence?

D2

Section structure and sequencing

Does the structure fit the paper type: IMRaD, literature review, theoretical, case study, policy brief? Are sections in a logical order, each building on what came before? Does the Introduction establish background, gap, and contribution? Does the Conclusion do more than restate?

D3

Argument logic and evidence chain

Does each major claim have adequate support? Are there gaps where a conclusion does not follow from the evidence? Are counter-arguments acknowledged where a reviewer would expect them? Does the Discussion interpret findings, or does it simply repeat the Results?

D4

Paragraph-level organization

Does each paragraph have one clear controlling idea? Does content stay on topic or drift? Are paragraphs in a sequence that builds logically? Are any so long they should be split, or so short they should be merged?

D5

Transitions and flow

Do section-to-section transitions signal the relationship between ideas, or does the reader need to guess? Do they do real work, or are they generic filler? Can a careful reader follow the logic from Introduction to Conclusion without losing the thread?

D6

Scope and balance

Is the Introduction proportionate to the rest of the paper? Is the Literature Review balanced across the field, or does it favour sources that support the conclusion? Is the Methods section detailed enough to allow replication? Is the Discussion proportionate to the Results?

D7

Conceptual clarity

Are key terms defined at first use and used consistently? Is jargon deployed purposefully, or is it obscuring the argument? Are figures and tables integrated into the argument, or floating separately without adequate interpretation?

Delivery

Comments in Word, ranked by priority

Developmental editing does not touch your text. It annotates it. Every finding is inserted as a native Word reviewer comment, anchored to the relevant section or paragraph. You read each comment in context and decide how to act on it.

  • A summary comment at the start of the document lists all findings by priority before you reach the individual annotations
  • Critical issues, which prevent the paper from achieving its purpose, appear first
  • Major issues, which reviewers are likely to flag, appear second
  • Minor issues, noticeable to careful readers, appear third
  • Each comment includes a specific observation and a specific, actionable recommendation
  • No text is rewritten: the revision decisions and the writing remain yours

Rate: €0.075 per word. Requires a complete draft. Developmental editing cannot assess argument structure or section balance from a partial manuscript. Submit the full paper before this service runs. If the draft is still in progress, academic writing may be the better starting point.

Scope

Structure and argument only

In scope

  • Thesis clarity and placement
  • Section structure and sequencing
  • Argument logic and evidence chain
  • Paragraph organization and topic sentences
  • Transitions between sections and paragraphs
  • Scope and section balance
  • Conceptual clarity and term consistency
  • Integration of figures and tables into the argument

Out of scope

  • Spelling, grammar, and punctuation
  • Citation format compliance
  • Sentence-level rewriting
  • Methodological validity or scientific accuracy
  • Statistical review

Surface corrections belong to proofreading. Run developmental editing first, then proofread the revised manuscript.

Service 03

Proofreading

A thorough final pass on surface errors: spelling, grammar, punctuation, citation compliance, and internal consistency. For manuscripts that are content-complete and ready to submit, pending a clean language check.

What gets checked

Eight categories, nothing missed

Proofreading at this level goes well beyond a grammar check. Citations get cross-referenced. Terminology gets checked for consistency across the full manuscript. Tables and figures get checked against every reference to them in the text.

1

Spelling and typographic errors

Misspellings, homophone confusion (affect/effect, principal/principle), inconsistent spelling of the same term across the manuscript, and OCR or paste artifacts from PDF imports.

2

Grammar and syntax

Subject-verb agreement, dangling and misplaced modifiers, incorrect verb tense and tense shifts within sections, article errors, run-on sentences, and comma splices. Ambiguous pronoun references are flagged as comments rather than silently changed.

3

Punctuation

Oxford comma consistency per your style guide, comma versus semicolon usage, colon and em dash spacing, quotation mark style, and hyphenation of compound modifiers.

4

Capitalization

Consistent capitalization of recurring terms, title case versus sentence case in headings, proper nouns, institutional names, and acronym first-use capitalization.

5

Internal consistency

Terminology used the same way throughout. Abbreviations introduced at first use and used consistently. Figures, tables, and footnotes numbered sequentially and correctly cross-referenced. Captions in consistent format.

6

Citations and references

In-text citation format compliance per your declared style. Reference list format compliance: field order, punctuation, italics, DOI formatting. Cross-check of every in-text citation against the reference list for orphan and phantom entries, year mismatches, name inconsistencies, and duplicates.

7

Numbers and data

Numeral versus word choice per your style guide, unit formatting, percentage symbol consistency, and verification of internal column totals and reported percentages in tables. Discrepancies are flagged as comments for your verification.

8

Formatting and layout

Paragraph spacing and indentation consistency, section heading hierarchy and spacing, and abstract word count flagged if over the limit you specify.

Supported citation styles

All major academic styles

Citation compliance checking requires a declared style. If your manuscript uses a style not listed here, get in touch before submitting.

APA 7th edition Chicago 17 — Author-Date Chicago 17 — Notes-Bibliography MLA 9th edition IEEE Vancouver

Delivery

Tracked changes in Word,
yours to accept or reject

All corrections are returned as native Word tracked changes. You review each one individually and accept or reject it using the standard Word review tools, exactly as you would with any other tracked-change document.

  • Clear-cut errors appear as tracked changes you accept or reject individually
  • Ambiguous cases, such as unclear pronoun references or citation mismatches requiring your verification, appear as comments explaining the issue and the suggested resolution
  • No changes are made silently: every correction is visible and reversible
  • Out-of-scope observations such as structural issues are noted briefly as comments and do not crowd out the language corrections

Rate: €0.025 per word. Rush rate: €0.05 per word (48-hour turnaround). Proofreading a draft that is still changing wastes effort. Submit only when the content, structure, and argument are settled. If the manuscript still needs structural work, developmental editing should run first.
